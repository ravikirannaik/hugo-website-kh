This paper examines inter-generational persistence of economic status and the role of assortative marriages in India. It investigates how marriage patterns -- particularly the tendency to marry within similar socioeconomic strata -- contribute to the reproduction of inequality across generations, analyzing the interplay between marriage markets and intergenerational mobility in the Indian context.
