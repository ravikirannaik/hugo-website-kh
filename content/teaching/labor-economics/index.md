---
title: "Labor Economics"
date: "2024-01-01"
---

Undergraduate course providing a comprehensive introduction to labor economics. Examines how labor markets function, how wages are determined, and how institutions shape employment outcomes. Draws on both theoretical frameworks and empirical evidence, with particular attention to labor market issues in developing economies.

Key topics: individual labor supply decisions, firm labor demand, competitive equilibrium, monopsony, human capital and returns to education, on-the-job training, education-occupation mismatch, wage differentials, discrimination, labor market institutions, informal employment, platform/gig economy, AI, automation, and the future of work.
