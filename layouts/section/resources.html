Platform Workers in India: Wages, Skills, and Working Conditions

This is a large-scale quantitative research project funded by the Indian Council of Social Science Research (ICSSR), 2022–ongoing. As Co-Project Director, I am studying how platform workers navigate precarity in India’s gig economy.

  • Field sites: Ahmedabad, Pune
  • Platforms studied: Zomato, Swiggy, Urban Company, Uber
  • Sample size: 2,000+ platform workers

Key research questions:

  • How do platform workers’ skills and specialization patterns affect earnings?
  • What determines wage differentials across heterogeneous platform types?
  • How do workers navigate algorithmic management and exercise agency?
  • What role does informality play in shaping platform work outcomes?

Methods include econometric analysis (wage determination, instrumental variables), large-scale primary survey data, and cross-platform comparative analysis.
